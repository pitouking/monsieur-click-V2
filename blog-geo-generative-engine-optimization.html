What is GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) and why should your business prepare for it today?

TL;DR – GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is about optimizing your business's digital presence so it's understood, cited and recommended by AI like ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude or Perplexity. It doesn't replace SEO. It's its natural evolution.

You've probably already used AI without realizing it: to write an email, plan a trip, compare two tools or simply ask a question.

Today, millions of people ask an AI directly which is the best tradesperson, which lawyer to choose or which SEO agency is the most reliable, before even going to Google. This shift completely changes how a business must build its visibility. It's exactly what we now call GEO.

What does GEO mean?

GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimization, the optimization of your business for generative search engines. In other words, the set of actions that let an AI understand who you are, what you do, why you're credible and why it can recommend you. The principle is simple: the more your business inspires trust, the more likely it is to be cited.

Does GEO replace SEO?

Absolutely not. SEO helps Google understand your site. GEO helps AI understand your business. But both rest on the same foundations.

SEOGEO
Get found on GoogleGet recommended by AI
Optimize search resultsOptimize generated answers
Attract visitorsBecome a source of trust
Rank pagesBuild authority

GEO therefore doesn't erase SEO. It adds a new dimension, in continuity with Google's ranking criteria.

Why doesn't AI cite every business?

An AI has to answer a question, but it can't recommend everyone. It therefore looks for the businesses that inspire the most trust, analyzing published content, reputation, reviews, citations, information consistency and brand authority. The more consistent your digital presence, the more credible your business becomes.

Keywords are no longer enough

For a long time, SEO mainly meant optimizing keywords. Today, modern engines seek above all to understand concepts. They want to identify your business, your services, your sector, your location and your expertise. They work with entities, not just keywords.

Your brand becomes an entity

The more consistently your brand appears on your site, on Google Business Profile, in directories, on professional networks and in specialized content, the more clearly AI identifies it, and the better it understands your business.

The mistake to avoid at all costs

Content must stay human

Many businesses think it's enough to ask ChatGPT to write articles. That's a mistake. AI doesn't reward auto-generated content: it favors useful content, real experiences, practical advice and businesses that demonstrate their expertise. AI is an assistant, not an author.

The 8 pillars of GEO

A fast site

A healthy technical base and flawless mobile display.

Educational content

Clear answers to your customers' real questions.

An editorial strategy

Regular publishing rather than one-off bursts.

Demonstrated expertise

Concrete proof of your know-how.

A maintained reputation

Authentic reviews and active follow-up.

A consistent identity

The same information everywhere across the web.

Structured data

Schema.org markup that clarifies your business.

An identifiable brand

A recognized entity cited by other sources.

When these eight elements work together, your visibility becomes much more solid, on Google as well as in AI answers.
