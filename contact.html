How to prepare for your audit

Nothing is required, but a few details make the conversation more useful. If you have them, keep your current website address, your Google Business Profile link, the cities and services you want to target, and an idea of the competitors who appear ahead of you on Google. Also note what "success" would concretely mean for you: more calls, more quote requests, more bookings, or an online image that matches the quality of your work. These markers let us be precise right away, but if you're starting from scratch, that's no problem: the audit exists precisely to lay the foundations.

The vocabulary, without jargon

So everything is clear from the start, here are the terms you'll hear. Local SEO is the set of actions that make you visible for location-based searches, like "your service near me". The Google Business Profile is your business listing on Google and Google Maps, often the first point of contact. NAP consistency (Name, Address, Phone) means showing exactly the same details everywhere, which builds Google's trust. Schema.org markup and OKF are structured data that describe your business in a language readable by engines and AI. Finally, GEO, or Generative Engine Optimization, prepares your presence so ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini and Perplexity can understand and cite you. You don't need to master any of this: it's our job, and we translate it for you at every step.
