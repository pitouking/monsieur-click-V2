Will artificial intelligence replace Google?

TL;DR – No, AI probably won't replace Google. But it's already transforming how people search for a business. Owners who keep thinking only 'Google SEO' risk missing a growing share of their future customers. The future belongs to businesses visible on Google, Google Maps and in AI answers at once.

"Is ChatGPT going to kill Google?" For two years, this question has come up in almost every conversation. The media regularly announce the end of search engines, and some owners wonder whether it's still worth investing in organic SEO.

Our answer is simple: no. But something even more important is happening. Search habits are changing, and that completely changes how a business must build its visibility.

What's really changing

For more than twenty years, the internet worked the same way: you had a question, you opened Google, you scanned several sites, you compared, then you decided.

Today, a new step appears. You ask your question directly to an AI: who's the best accountant for a small business? Which SEO consultant should I choose? Which roofer is recommended near me? Instead of getting ten links, you get a direct answer.

Google is no longer alone

For a long time, Google held almost all the attention. Today, people use several tools depending on their needs.

NeedTool often used
Find a restaurantGoogle Maps
Compare businessesGoogle
Understand a topicChatGPT
Do in-depth researchPerplexity
Get a quick summaryGemini

Tomorrow, these uses will keep blending. The visible business will be the one present everywhere its future customers look for an answer.

AI doesn't replace Google, it changes the customer journey

Yesterday, the journey was short: Google, then the site, then the quote request. Today, it lengthens and trust starts earlier.

  1. Ask an AI

    ChatGPT, Gemini or Perplexity give a first summary and a few names.

  2. Check on Google

    The prospect digs deeper and compares the cited businesses.

  3. Consult Google Maps

    They spot nearby pros and their ratings.

  4. Read the reviews

    They look for social proof before committing.

  5. Visit the site

    They confirm the expertise and reassure themselves.

  6. Get in touch

    The decision is already largely made.

The same fundamentals keep winning

Good news: businesses that already work on their visibility don't start from scratch. AI appreciates almost the same criteria as Google.

Demonstrated expertise

Prove your know-how rather than claim it.

Useful content

Clearly answer customers' questions.

A solid reputation

Authentic reviews and a consistent local presence.

Reliable information

The same identity and data everywhere.

In other words, doing good SEO today already prepares your AI visibility of tomorrow.

The real change is strategic, not technical

The question is no longer "How do I be first on Google?" but "Why would an AI recommend my business over another?". This nuance changes everything: you're no longer only working on your SEO, you're building your authority. It's already what pushes your customers to ask ChatGPT who to choose.

The 7 mistakes that will cost you ground

Thinking SEO is dead. Stopping publishing content. Replacing all your content with auto-generated text. Neglecting Google Business Profile. Forgetting customer reviews. Believing an AI recommends at random. Waiting for your competitors to get ahead.
